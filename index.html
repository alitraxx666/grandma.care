Phase One Prototype

The first version of Grandma.care uses two connected apps: a caregiver app for a family member’s phone and a senior tablet app designed to run throughout the day on an iPad, Android tablet, or older dedicated tablet.

Families can place the tablet somewhere visible in the home so reminders, check-ins, and family messages are easy for the senior to see and respond to. The goal is to make support feel calm, familiar, and simple.

Future Vision

Future versions of Grandma.care may expand beyond standard tablets into a dedicated senior-friendly device: simple, portable, always-on, and designed specifically for care reminders, safety check-ins, and family connection at home or on the go.

The Story Behind Grandma.care

Grandma.care began with a real grandmother.

As our family helped support an elderly loved one experiencing memory and cognitive challenges, we discovered how difficult it can be to balance independence with safety. We wanted her to continue living in her own home, maintain her routines, and stay connected with family while still having a simple way to check in and offer support when needed.

Existing technology often felt too complicated, too clinical, or simply was not designed for older adults who are not comfortable with modern devices. That experience inspired the creation of Grandma.care.

Grandma.care is being built to help seniors remain independent for as long as possible while giving families greater peace of mind. Every reminder, check-in, and feature is guided by a simple goal: helping loved ones stay safe, connected, and supported without sacrificing dignity or independence.

This is not a hypothetical project. It was inspired by a real family, a real grandmother, and the everyday challenges that millions of families face as they care for aging loved ones.
